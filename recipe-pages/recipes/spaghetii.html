Classic Spaghetti with Tomato Sauce Recipe

Spaghetti

Spaghetti is a long, thin, cylindrical pasta that hails from Italy, renowned for its versatility and delightful texture. Typically made from durum wheat semolina, spaghetti is characterized by its firm yet tender bite when cooked al dente. This beloved pasta serves as a canvas for a myriad of sauces, from rich and hearty marinara to creamy Alfredo, and can be paired with a variety of ingredients, including meats, vegetables, and herbs. Often garnished with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and aromatic basil, spaghetti is a comforting dish enjoyed by people of all ages, making it a staple in households and restaurants worldwide.

Ingredients:

For the Spaghetti

  • 8 oz (225g) spaghetti pasta
  • Salt (for boiling water)

For the Tomato Sauce

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes (or whole peeled tomatoes)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil (or a handful of fresh basil, chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Parmesan cheese, for serving (optional)
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish (optional)
